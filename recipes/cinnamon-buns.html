Cinnamon Buns

Arguably earth's greatest accomplishment

Gooey cinnamon-flavoured goodness? Check. Eat at your own risk.

Adapted from thestayathomechef.com

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tbsp instant dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp butter (salted, softened)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 1⁄2 cups bread flour

Filling

  • 1⁄2 butter (salted, melted)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • Ground cinnamon to taste

Frosting

  • Cream cheese to taste
  • 1⁄4 cup butter (salted, softened)
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1⁄2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1-2 tbsp milk

Instructions

  1. Heat milk and add the yeast. Let stand until yeast begins to rise.
  2. Using a stand mixer, combine yeast mixture, sugar, salt, butter, and eggs. Incorporate flour in portions using a dough hook with low speed.
  3. Once flour has been incorporated, increase the speed to medium. Add more flour as necessary, dough should be tacky.
  4. Let dough stand in a greased bowl until roughly doubled in size.
  5. Lightly grease a casserole dish. Roll the dough out on a floured surface into a 12 x 18 inch rectangle.
  6. Brush the dough with the melted butter. Sprinkle brown sugar and cinnamon evenly across the surface of the dough. Purely base this on vibes.
  7. Tightly roll the dough lengthwise. Cut into even pieces as desired.
  8. Place slices onto the greased pan. Cover and let rise.
  9. Preheat oven to 325 degrees (F). Bake for about 14 minutes, or until lightly golden on top
  10. While the rolls are baking, make the frosting by mixing all the frosting ingredients using a hand mixer. Mixture should have a drizzle-like consistency.
  11. Frost the rolls while still warm. Eat the entire pan. (repeat)
